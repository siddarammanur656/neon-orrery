The Space

The Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun, supporting life with water, atmosphere, diverse ecosystems, and a dynamic climate.

The Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite, orbiting at an average distance of about 384,400 kilometers.

Solar System

solar system consists of the Sun, eight planets, moons, asteroids, and comets, orbiting in space.

The Stars

Stars are luminous spheres of gas, primarily hydrogen and helium, generating energy through nuclear fusion, powering galaxies.

Black Hole

A black hole is a region in space with intense gravity, where nothing, not even light, can escape its pull.

The Galaxy

A galaxy is a massive collection of stars, planets, gas, and dark matter, held together by gravity, spanning vast distances.

The Nebula

A nebula is a vast cloud of gas and dust in space, often a stellar nursery where stars are born.

Near-Earth Asteroids

Asteroid 2020 VT4

Asteroid 2020 VT4 flew closer to Earth than many satellites, coming within 400 kilometers, but its small size meant it would have likely disintegrated if it had entered the atmosphere.

Asteroid 2004 FH

Asteroid 2004 FH is a small near-Earth object that measures about 30 meters in diameter. It made a notably close approach to Earth on March 18, 2004, passing just 43,000 kilometers away.

Asteroid 99942 Apophis

Asteroid 99942 Apophis, approximately 340 meters in diameter, will pass within about 31,000 kilometers of Earth on April 13, 2029. Initially considered a potential impact threat, its risk has been significantly reduced through ongoing observations. This close approach underscores the importance of monitoring near-Earth objects for planetary defense.

Comet C2023 A3

Comet C/2023 A3 (Tsuchinshan–ATLAS) will make its closest approach to Earth on October 12, 2024, at a distance of about 44 million miles (71 million km). It passed its closest point to the Sun on September 27 and is expected to brighten dramatically in mid-October.

Comet-13POlbers

Comet 13P/Olbers, discovered in 1815, will reach its closest point to the Sun on June 30, 2024, and is expected to brighten to around magnitude 6.5, making it visible to the naked eye. Its orbital period is about 69 years, and it last appeared in 1956.

Comet C2012 S1 (ISON)

Comet C/2012 S1 (ISON) was a sungrazing comet discovered in September 2012, anticipated to be very bright during its close approach to the Sun on November 28, 2013. Unfortunately, it disintegrated due to intense solar heat and gravitational forces, failing to become the spectacular comet many hoped for. Despite its demise, ISON provided valuable data about cometary behavior and the solar system.

Comet C2020 F3(NEOWISE)

Comet C/2020 F3 (NEOWISE), discovered in March 2020, became highly visible in July, offering stunning views as one of the brightest comets in decades. It made its closest approach to the Sun on July 3, 2020, and was observable to the naked eye in the Northern Hemisphere during early mornings and evenings.

Potentially Hazardous Asteroids

1950DA

1950 DA is a Near-Earth Potentially Hazardous Asteroid, roughly 1.3 km in diameter, with a potential risk of impact in the year 2880. It has one of the highest known impact probabilities, though the chance remains extremely low.

2022 EB5

2022 EB5 was a small Near-Earth Potentially Hazardous Asteroid that impacted Earth's atmosphere on March 11, 2022, over the Norwegian Sea. It was detected just hours before impact, making it the fifth asteroid discovered prior to hitting Earth.

1994 PC1

1994 PC1 is a large Near-Earth Potentially Hazardous Asteroid, approximately 1.1 km in diameter. It passed safely by Earth on January 18, 2022, at a distance of about 1.9 million kilometers, posing no immediate threat.

2023 TL4

2023 TL4 is a Near-Earth Object (NEO) classified as a Potentially Hazardous Asteroid (PHA) due to its proximity to Earth. Though it poses no immediate threat, such objects are monitored closely for any future risk of collision.

(153814) 2001 WN5

(153814) 2001 WN5 is a large Near-Earth Potentially Hazardous Asteroid, approximately 1 km in diameter. It is expected to pass within 250,000 km of Earth in 2028, which is closer than the distance to the moon. Despite its proximity, current assessments indicate no significant threat of impact.

Gravity

In space, gravity is the main force that pulls objects towards each other. It keeps planets moving around stars, moons circling planets, and helps shape galaxies. Everything with mass has gravity, but bigger objects like stars and planets have a stronger pull. Gravity also bends light, which can create interesting effects like gravitational lensing. It’s also very important in the life of stars, helping them form, change over time, and eventually collapse.
