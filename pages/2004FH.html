Asteroid 2004 FH

Asteroid 2004 FH is another small NEA that gained attention for its close approach to Earth. This asteroid, which is about 30 meters (100 feet) in diameter, passed by Earth on March 18, 2004, at a distance of only 43,000 kilometers (26,500 miles), which is about one-tenth the distance between Earth and the Moon. Though small, an asteroid this size could cause significant localized damage if it were to enter Earth’s atmosphere and explode. However, like many NEAs of this size, 2004 FH passed safely by without incident. Its close approach was an important reminder of the need for early detection systems like NASA’s Near-Earth Object program, which continuously scans the sky for potentially hazardous asteroids.
