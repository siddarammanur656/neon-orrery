The Nebula

A nebula is a vast cloud of gas and dust in space, often serving as a stellar nursery where new stars are born. Nebulae come in various forms: emission nebulae glow brightly due to the ionization of their gases by nearby young stars, while reflection nebulae do not emit their own light but reflect the light of stars. Dark nebulae, on the other hand, are dense clouds that obscure the light from stars or other nebulae behind them. Planetary nebulae are shells of gas and plasma that are ejected by dying stars in the late stages of their evolution. One of the most famous nebulae is the Orion Nebula, which is visible to the naked eye and is one of the brightest in the night sky. Nebulae are often associated with regions of intense star formation, and the material within them eventually coalesces to form new stars and planetary systems.
