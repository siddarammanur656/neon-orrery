Asteroid 99942 Apophis

Asteroid 99942 Apophis is one of the most famous Near-Earth Asteroids due to the initial concern that it could collide with Earth in the future. Apophis was discovered in 2004, and early calculations suggested that there was a small but significant chance of impact during a close approach in 2029. This possibility led to widespread media attention and Apophis being dubbed the "doomsday asteroid." Apophis is about 340 meters (1,100 feet) in diameter, and an impact from an asteroid of this size would have catastrophic regional effects. This will be a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to observe an asteroid of this size passing so close to Earth, and it will provide a wealth of scientific data. While the chance of a future impact with Apophis has been ruled out for the foreseeable future, its close approach will still be a spectacular astronomical event.
