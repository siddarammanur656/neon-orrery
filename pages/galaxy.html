The Galaxy

Galaxies are immense systems composed of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter, all bound together by gravity. They come in various shapes and sizes, including spiral, elliptical, and irregular forms. Spiral galaxies, like our Milky Way, have well-defined arms that rotate around a central bulge. Elliptical galaxies are more spherical or oval-shaped and contain older stars, while irregular galaxies lack a clear structure, often the result of galactic collisions. Galaxies vary greatly in size, with some dwarf galaxies containing only a few billion stars, while giant galaxies can house trillions of stars. The Milky Way itself is home to about 100-400 billion stars, and our nearest neighbor, the Andromeda Galaxy, is on a collision course with the Milky Way, with the two expected to merge in about 4.5 billion years.
