Comet C2012 S1

Comet C/2012 S1 (ISON), often referred to simply as Comet ISON, was discovered in 2012 and gained widespread attention due to predictions that it could become one of the brightest comets in history. ISON was a "sungrazing" comet, meaning its orbit took it extremely close to the Sun. Unfortunately, as ISON made its closest approach to the Sun in November 2013, it disintegrated due to the intense solar heat and gravitational forces. Despite its dramatic breakup, Comet ISON provided valuable data for scientists studying how comets react to the extreme conditions near the Sun. Its demise also served as a reminder that not all comets survive their close encounters with our star.
